Permissible & Non-Permissible in Islam

About This Website

This website is an educational project created to explain Islamic concepts like Halal and Haram in a simple and easy-to-understand way for students and anyone interested in learning basic Islamic teachings.

It also explains other important terms like Makruh, Mubah, and Mustahabb, along with references from the Qur’an, Hadith, and Islamic Fiqh literature. The goal is to provide knowledge and awareness about permissible and non-permissible actions in Islam.

Halal (Permissible)

Halal means anything that is allowed in Islam according to Shariah law. Muslims are encouraged to consume and perform actions that are halal. This includes food, drink, clothing, and ethical behavior.

Reference: Qur’an 2:168

Haram (Prohibited)

Haram refers to actions or items that are strictly forbidden in Islam. Performing haram actions is considered sinful. Examples include consuming alcohol, gambling, and stealing.

Reference: Qur’an 5:3

Makruh (Disliked)

Makruh refers to actions that are discouraged in Islam. Avoiding them is better, but doing them is not a major sin. Example: wasting water while performing Wudu.

Reference: Islamic Fiqh Literature

Mubah (Neutral)

Mubah refers to actions that are neutral in Islam. Performing or avoiding them does not result in reward or punishment. Example: choosing what color of clothes to wear.

Reference: Usul al-Fiqh

Mustahabb (Recommended)

Mustahabb actions are recommended in Islam. Performing them brings reward, but not doing them is not sinful. Example: voluntary prayers (Sunnah prayers).

Reference: Sahih Hadith Literature

References

  • Qur’an 2:168 – Regarding Halal food and ethics
  • Qur’an 5:3 – Prohibition of Haram actions
  • Sahih Bukhari & Sahih Muslim – Authentic Hadith
  • Islamic Fiqh Literature – Explanation of Makruh, Mubah, Mustahabb
  • Usul al-Fiqh – Principles of Islamic law
